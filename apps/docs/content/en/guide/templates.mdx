Once the content is roughly in, make it look good. **Changing templates only affects the look, never your content** — try freely.

## Change the template

<Steps>
<Step title="Open the Template panel">
In the editor, find the **Template** panel. It shows a row of templates to choose from.
</Step>
<Step title="Pick one and apply">
Click the one you like, then click **Change template** — the preview on the right updates instantly.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Customize the styling

Want to fine-tune? Click **Customize Template** (or **Customize** in the panel). Common options:

- **Layout**: **Single Column** or **Two Column**.
- **Fonts, colors, spacing**, and other details. ⚠️ To verify visually: the exact options depend on what the panel shows.

<Tip>
**Two-column** is more compact and suits content-heavy resumes; **single-column** is cleaner and friendlier to ATS (resume screening systems). Not sure? Start with single-column.
</Tip>

<Note>
Switch as much as you like — **content and appearance are separate**. The template decides how it looks; your text never changes.
</Note>
